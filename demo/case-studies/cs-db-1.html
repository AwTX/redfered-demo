Case Study

Lakehouse Migration

01Overview

A financial services firm operated separate data lake and data warehouse infrastructure that had diverged over five years, resulting in duplicated datasets, inconsistent transformation logic, and a two-tier analyst experience where different teams worked from different versions of the truth.

02Problem

Data engineers spent significant time keeping the lake and warehouse in sync. Discrepancies between the two systems caused recurring conflicts in board-level reporting. The dual infrastructure had a combined annual cost exceeding $8M.

03Solution

A lakehouse migration was executed, consolidating all analytical workloads onto a single open-format platform. Existing ETL jobs were migrated and refactored to use declarative transformation frameworks. A medallion architecture was adopted to enforce data quality gates at each processing stage.

04Results

1

Unified data platform

$4.1M

Annual infrastructure savings

100%

Reporting consistency across teams

The consolidated lakehouse eliminated the data consistency issues that had plagued the firm for years. Data engineers now focus on building new capabilities rather than maintaining synchronization between systems.

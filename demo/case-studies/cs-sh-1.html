Case Study

Headless Commerce Migration

01Overview

A global fashion retailer needed to migrate from a monolithic e-commerce platform to a headless architecture to support rapid international expansion, custom storefronts for each market, and faster frontend iteration without backend constraints.

02Problem

The monolithic platform required full deployments for any frontend change, slowing the design team's ability to iterate. Different markets required localisation features the platform couldn't support without expensive custom development. Mobile performance scores were consistently poor.

03Solution

A headless architecture was implemented with a React-based storefront consuming the commerce API. Market-specific storefronts were built as separate Next.js applications sharing a component library. Server-side rendering improved performance, and a visual editing layer allowed the marketing team to manage content independently.

04Results

4×

Faster frontend deployments

90+

Lighthouse performance score

14%

Conversion rate uplift

The headless architecture unlocked a level of frontend agility previously impossible. The marketing team can now launch localised campaigns without engineering involvement, dramatically reducing time-to-market.

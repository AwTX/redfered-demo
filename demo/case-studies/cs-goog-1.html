Case Study

ML-Powered Search Ranking

01Overview

An e-commerce platform's product search relied on hand-tuned heuristic ranking rules that had accumulated over a decade. The rules were brittle, hard to maintain, and increasingly misaligned with user behavior as the product catalog expanded to over 5 million SKUs.

02Problem

Search relevance had stagnated, with 23% of search sessions ending without a click — a strong signal that results were failing users. The heuristic rules required weeks of expert tuning for each new product category, making expansion costly.

03Solution

A learning-to-rank model was trained on historical click and purchase signals, replacing the heuristic system. A/B testing infrastructure was built to evaluate ranking experiments safely. The model was retrained weekly on fresh behavioral data and served with sub-10ms latency via an optimized inference layer.

04Results

18%

Click-through rate increase

11%

Conversion rate uplift

23→9%

Zero-click sessions

The ML ranking system significantly outperforms the legacy heuristics across all product categories. The self-improving nature of the model means relevance continues to increase as more behavioral data accumulates.

Case Study

GPU-Accelerated Model Training

01Overview

A computer vision startup was spending 4–6 days training production models on CPU-based infrastructure, severely limiting the team's ability to experiment and iterate. The project aimed to migrate training workloads to GPU-accelerated infrastructure and optimise the training pipeline.

02Problem

Long training cycles created a feedback loop that slowed research velocity. Researchers ran fewer experiments, favored conservative architectural choices, and struggled to meet product deadlines. Cloud GPU costs were poorly managed, often idling between jobs.

03Solution

Training infrastructure was migrated to GPU clusters with spot-instance cost optimisation. The training pipeline was refactored to support mixed-precision training, gradient accumulation, and distributed data parallelism across multiple GPUs. An experiment tracking platform was introduced to manage the increased pace of experimentation.

04Results

18×

Training speed improvement

4 hours

Avg. training cycle (down from 5 days)

40%

GPU cost reduction

Researchers now iterate at a pace previously impossible, running dozens of experiments per week. Model quality has improved as a direct result of more thorough hyperparameter exploration.

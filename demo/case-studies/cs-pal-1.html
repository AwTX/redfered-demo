Case Study

Supply Chain Risk Intelligence

01Overview

A multinational manufacturer needed visibility into supply chain risks across thousands of suppliers spanning 60 countries. Single-source dependencies, geopolitical risks, and natural disaster exposure were poorly understood, leaving the company vulnerable to costly disruptions.

02Problem

Supplier risk data was fragmented across procurement systems, spreadsheets, and informal knowledge. The 2021 shipping disruptions cost the company $200M in lost production due to component shortages that were not anticipated or visible until they had already materialised.

03Solution

A supply chain risk intelligence platform was built, integrating internal procurement data with external signals including news, logistics data, financial health indicators, and geopolitical risk scores. An interactive risk map allowed procurement teams to identify single-source dependencies and simulate disruption scenarios.

04Results

6 weeks

Avg. disruption early warning

73%

Single-source dependencies reduced

$140M

Disruption costs avoided in year 1

The company successfully diversified its most critical supplier dependencies before several predicted disruptions materialised. The early warning capability has become a strategic advantage in procurement negotiations.

Case Study

Enterprise Knowledge Management

01Overview

A global enterprise struggled with institutional knowledge fragmented across wikis, SharePoint sites, and email threads. Employees spent significant time searching for internal documentation, reducing productivity and slowing onboarding for new hires.

02Problem

No single source of truth existed for internal processes, policies, or technical documentation. Keyword search returned thousands of irrelevant results, and critical institutional knowledge lived exclusively in the heads of long-tenured employees.

03Solution

A semantic search system was built on top of the existing document repositories, using vector embeddings to understand intent rather than keywords. An internal assistant interface allowed employees to ask natural-language questions and receive direct answers with source citations. Content was automatically re-indexed on a nightly schedule.

04Results

70%

Search time reduction

3 days

Onboarding time saved per hire

91%

Employee satisfaction score

Knowledge retrieval is now fast and accurate, enabling employees to self-serve on most queries without escalating to subject matter experts. Onboarding programs were redesigned around the new tool.

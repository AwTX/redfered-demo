Case Study

Collaborative Analytics for Healthcare

01Overview

A consortium of healthcare networks needed to collaborate on population health analytics across combined patient datasets, while ensuring strict compliance with data privacy regulations that prohibited raw data from leaving each organisation's controlled environment.

02Problem

Valuable population-level insights were locked within individual health network siloes. Research collaborations required lengthy legal negotiations and physical data transfers with complex de-identification processes, taking months to establish and limiting the scope of studies.

03Solution

A privacy-preserving collaboration architecture was implemented allowing each network to contribute to joint analyses without sharing raw patient records. Query results were the only data exchanged, with differential privacy techniques applied to prevent re-identification. Data governance controls ensured each participant could audit exactly what computations were performed on their data.

04Results

6 weeks

Time to first joint analysis (down from 9 months)

0

Raw patient records shared

12

New research studies enabled in year 1

The consortium now conducts population health research at a scale previously impossible, unlocking insights that benefit patient outcomes across the network. The privacy-preserving architecture has become a template for future research collaborations.
